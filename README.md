# CS 250 Portfolio Artifact

## Artifact

This repository contains my Sprint Review and Retrospective from CS 250: Software Development Lifecycle. This project demonstrates my understanding of Agile and Scrum practices, including sprint planning, sprint reviews, retrospectives, user stories, and Agile development principles.

## Reflection

### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by understanding the customer's goals and translating them into software requirements. Creating user stories helps organize those requirements from the user's perspective, allowing the development team to focus on delivering valuable features while keeping customer needs at the center of the project.

### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach software development by breaking projects into smaller, manageable tasks and continuously improving the product through feedback and testing. Agile has taught me the importance of sprint planning, maintaining a product backlog, participating in daily Scrum meetings, conducting sprint reviews, and reflecting during retrospectives. I plan to continue using these practices in future software development projects.

### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating effectively, completing assigned work on time, collaborating with others, and being open to feedback. Successful Agile teams depend on trust, accountability, and continuous communication to deliver quality software.
